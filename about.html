About

Hey, how do you say your name???

It's like saying [SURE] and [REEN]. Now, say it 10 times fast!

Hi, I'm Sharynne. I am a student at the University of Kansas with a degree in Psychology and a pending degree in Computer Science. I started off college with hopes and dreams of becoming a pediatrician, but I was turned away by the dog-eat-dog culture of the industry. So, I thought it would be a better idea to stay in academia and pursue a PhD in child psychology.

I thought wrong.

In my last semester before graduating, I enrolled in an elective programming class because I believed it would help me in my research with data analysis. Turns out, I really enjoyed the class.

Really really enjoyed it.

It became nonstop. I signed up for courses on websites like Udacity and Codecademy, watched hours and hours of YouTube tutorials, and read forums until dawn. My friends did not understand how I can enjoy such a "boring" task. Even those who are programmers themselves!

Me neither.

Though, as I thought about it, everything made sense. Since I was in elementary school, I liked making things. I remembered making RC cars out of my jewelry box when I was 9 years old. I remembered selling modified mechanical pencils to classmates when I was 11 years old. Long story short, there was a sort of a proud satisfaction in seeing things take shape in front of my eyes. With coding, I didn't need material or a huge lab to make something. I only need a small machine and I can turn simple lines of code into something amazing that everyone can enjoy.

I was so excited to share my enthusiasm for programming with other people. So, I combined my two passions - helping children and coding - and co-founded DevelopECBC in 2015 to teach kids how to code. It has been extremely inspiring to witness their creativity and curiosity.

Outside programming, I am a big foodie. I like trying out new restaurants, eating and cooking new foods, and taking lots and lots of pictures of it. Anything from street food to fine dining. Follow my adventures on Instagram!
