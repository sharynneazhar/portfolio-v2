I am a Computer Science student at the University of Kansas (KU), a software engineering intern at PayIt, and a co-founder of DevelopECBC. I am passionate about designing and developing software that is both innovative and impactful. My main fields of interest are in web and mobile development as well as in design and user experience.

I found curiosity in programming in my junior year of college in an R-based statistics class. I was so amazed by how a few simple lines of code could turn boring data sets into cool graphical displays. I ended up spending a lot of time on learning how to use R than the intended material for the class. Guilty! Eventually, I wanted to learn more about how programming works fundamentally. How can I make my own programs like this? How can I make my life easier with programs like this?

In the next semester, I enrolled in an introductory C++ course. I was so proud of my first “Hello World” program. I showed everyone what I did. I became obsessed with programming. In just a few months, I crunched through several Codecademy courses, read over a bunch of programming subreddits, and watched many Youtube tutorials. I even watched some live streams of people programming.

Long story short, I had decided to go back to school for Computer Science. Since then, I have worked for KU as a web developer, co-founded a program to teach kids how to code, and earned a nanodegree from Udacity. Whether it's learning how to code or teaching how to code, I love to get my hands on a keyboard and seeing how things come together.

Outside programming, I am a big foodie and a slightly above-average cook. I am also a Youth Leader at Emmanuel Chinese Baptist Church. You can follow my adventures on Instagram!
